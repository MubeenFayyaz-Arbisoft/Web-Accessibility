List Type Demo

Usage of <ul> in HTML:

Unordered Lists (<ul>) are used for items that don't require a specific order, typically marked with bullet points. 

              
                <ul>
                  <li>Contents</li>
                  <li>Intro</li>
                  <li>Previous: Technique H46</li>
                  <li>Next: Technique H49</li>
                </ul>

Usage of <ol> in HTML:

Ordered Lists (<ol>) are used for items that have a specific sequence, typically numbered.

              
                <ol>
                  <li>Contents</li>
                  <li>Intro</li>
                  <li>Previous</li>
                  <li>Next</li>
                </ol>

Usage of <dl> in HTML:

Description Lists (<dl>): Used for pairs of terms and descriptions, consisting of <dt> for the term and <dd> for the description. Example: 

              
                <dl>
                  <dt>Coffee</dt>
                  <dd>Black Hot Drink</dd>
                  <dt>Milk</dt>
                  <dd>White Cold Drink</dd>
                </dl>
