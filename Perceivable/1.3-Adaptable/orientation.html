1.3.4 Orientation

Explanation:

The WCAG Orientation guideline ensures that users can access content in both portrait and landscape modes unless a specific orientation is essential for functionality. This is particularly important for providing a flexible and inclusive user experience, as individuals with disabilities may have a device fixed in a certain orientation due to assistive technology, physical constraints, or mounting setups.

For example, a user with a motor impairment might mount their device in a wheelchair or on a stationary stand, making it impossible to switch orientations. In such scenarios, restricting content to a single orientation can create significant barriers to access.

Additionally, when the orientation changes, all content and functionality must remain fully accessible, ensuring no critical information is cut off or hidden. There should also be no horizontal scrolling unless absolutely necessary (e.g., for specific content like data tables or maps). Excessive horizontal scrolling can significantly hinder usability, especially for users with motor impairments or those relying on assistive technologies.
