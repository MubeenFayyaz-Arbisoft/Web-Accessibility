1.3.5 Identify Input Purpose

Usage in HTML:

The type and autocomplete attributes are essential for ensuring accessibility in forms. The type attribute specifies the expected input format for a field, such as text, email, date, or password, helping both users and assistive technologies understand the type of information to be entered. For example, a field with type="email" indicates to users that an email address is expected, while assistive technologies can announce it accordingly. The autocomplete attribute, on the other hand, enables browsers and assistive tools to provide suggestions or autofill options based on the user's previous entries. Fields with specific autocomplete values, such as autocomplete="name", autocomplete="email", or autocomplete="bday", assist users in quickly and accurately filling out forms, reducing cognitive load. These attributes are crucial for users with disabilities, particularly those who rely on screen readers or other assistive technologies, as they help clearly identify the purpose of each input field and provide better guidance, improving the overall user experience and ensuring the form is usable by everyone. 

              
<form>
  <label for="name">Full Name</label>
  <input type="text" id="name" name="name" autocomplete="name" placeholder="Enter your full name">

  <label for="email">Email Address</label>
  <input type="email" id="email" name="email" autocomplete="email" placeholder="Enter your email address">

  <label for="dob">Date of Birth</label>
  <input type="date" id="dob" name="dob" autocomplete="bday">

  <label for="address">Street Address</label>
  <input type="text" id="address" name="address" autocomplete="street-address" placeholder="Enter your address">

  <button type="submit">Submit</button>
</form>
