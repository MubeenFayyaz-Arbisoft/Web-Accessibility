Guideline 1.1/Captcha-Alternatives

Usage of audio CAPTCHA in HTML:

Explanation:

Audio CAPTCHA (<audio> tag):
The <audio> element allows users to listen to an audio CAPTCHA challenge. The src="captcha-audio.mp3" points to the audio file that contains the CAPTCHA challenge.
You'll need to generate and store the audio file dynamically on your server.
The controls attribute enables the user to play, pause, and adjust the volume of the audio.

ARIA Labeling:

The aria-labelledby attribute on the <audio> element links the audio player to the text instruction: "Type the two pieces of text"
The aria-labelledby attribute on the <input> field links the input field to its label 

              
                <!-- Image for captcha-->
                    <img src="two pieces words.jpg" alt="captcha words">
                    
              <!-- CAPTCHA input field -->

                     <div class="captcha-container">
                     <label for="captcha-input" id="captcha-input-label">Type the two pieces of text:</label>
                     <input type="text" id="captcha-input" name="captcha-input"class="captcha-input" aria-labelledby="captcha-input-label"required>
                     </div>
                
              <!-- Audio CAPTCHA -->

                    <audio controls aria-labelledby="captcha-label">
                    <source src="captcha-audio.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
                        Your browser will speak the audio element.
                        </audio>
                        </div>

Usage of reCAPTCHA in HTML:

Explanation:

As Keyboard can focus on the checkbox and check it, the alternative is to provide an image along with an audio to speak out the written text. Which in result will help low vision people to hear the text written and overcome the CAPTCHA. Along with the reCAPTCHA checkbox and alternative audio is given, where you can hear the written text and type it in the text box. To create your own reCaptcha please visit: reCAPTCHA site link 

              
                <---Add "script src="" above the <head> tag in html for the reCaptcha block to appear--->
                <script src="https://www.google.com/recaptcha/api.js" async defer></script>
                


                <div class="g-recaptcha" data-sitekey="your_site_key"></div>
                <---The data-siteKey is generated from Google reCaptcha insert this within your form div--->

Usage of CAPTCHA in HTML for multiple image:

Explanation:

For each checkbox given, an alternative text should be provided on voice over (which should describe the image) to help low vision people help pass the CAPTCHA of multiple image selection.
